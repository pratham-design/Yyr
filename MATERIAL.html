Materials

Steel

Steel is a commonly used material in car manufacturing due to its strength, durability, and affordability.

Key features:

  • High tensile strength
  • Excellent impact resistance
  • Easy to shape and mold

Aluminum

Aluminum is valued for its lightweight properties, making it ideal for components that require reduced weight without sacrificing strength.

Key features:

  • Low density
  • High corrosion resistance
  • Conductive properties

Carbon Fiber

Carbon fiber is a high-performance material known for its exceptional strength-to-weight ratio, making it popular in sports cars and high-end vehicles.

Key features:

  • Lightweight and rigid
  • Resistant to fatigue and deformation
  • Highly customizable

Plastics

Various types of plastics are used in car manufacturing for their versatility, cost-effectiveness, and ability to be molded into complex shapes.

Key features:

  • Lightweight
  • Corrosion-resistant
  • Impact-absorbing properties
